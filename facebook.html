I worked as a product design intern in Enterprise Team this past summer. It was a wonderful summer! Able to enjoy the bay area’s weather, meet with awesome designers, engineers! More importantly, move fast and grow fast. I’m unable to share the details of my projects due to NDA. But I’d love to share some key learnings I have during this internship:



KEY LEARNINGS


Have strong opinions about your design but hold weakly
Everyone in Facebook like to provide feedback and everyone has an opinion for design. When people give comments or criticisms to the design, ask clarification questions to figure out ‘why’ behind it.



Most fast means iterate fast
Move fast does not mean I need to finish and finalize all decisions by myself pretty fast, instead, it means I can work with the team and iterate fast (we can settle down one design and implement it, and then change to other alternatives if needed)



Provide the idealist solution even if everyone says it’s not possible
Sometimes people will tell you just come up with the visual design because of time constraints. But if designers find some key interactions need to change, always push limits to come up with the ideal solution.
Don’t be afraid to bring up with wild ideas but also keep in mind the engineering efforts.



People are busy but people are willing to help
If we need someone to unblock the move, tell others about when and what you need before-hand. At the same time, don’t be afraid to reach out to others as long as you find the right person. I reached out to different roles and other teams to talk about my ideas, sometimes people will give you much more support than you expected and that’s when you can make more impact together.



Overcommunication is good
Increase the transparency of your work. Communicate with your team or outside your team about your wild ideas. Sometimes it will be a surprise Also when you seek for some feedback, communicate your takeaways of the feedback. Communication is the key to efficiency.



In the end, best thanks to my team especially my dear mentor Jamie Tseng who provides me best support in my internship! Thank you!
