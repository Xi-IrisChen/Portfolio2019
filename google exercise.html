OVERVIEW



BACKGROUND

There are 450 organizations In Georgia Tech. Most students will only explore new student organizations at the activity fair, which is a huge gathering for all student organizations at the beginning of the semester. And many students may join a lot of organizations at the beginning, but only left 1-2 in the end. Whether because they forget it or the organization is not active not all.

For proposing experience, about 20-30 students will attempt to propose a student organization. But only about 5-10 students get approved. The process of proposing is really complicated. And it always comes to problem like hard to find an advisor or similar organizations already exist...

UNDERSTANDING

INTERVIEW

As the user group is new students who want to join or propose a student organization, I started by a semi-structured interview for my classmates who had related experience before. The objective for the interview mainly including:
- What’s their motivation for joining?
- How to find the organization they’re interested in?

I recruited 6 people who all had joining experience before. However, only one student had proposing experience. When I tried to reach out to other students, I still found few students had related experience. Even they had, they had vague impression and cannot remember the details. So I decided to look for some help at the Students Center for Student Engagement (which was responsible for managing student organizaiton in Georgia Tech) and conducted a stakeholder interview with associates dean finally.




'BROWSE', 'SEARCH' EXPERIENCE

For total there are 450+ organizations. Students mainly have 4 ways to discover student organization. Almost everyone participated in the activity fair. Also a lot joined the organization later by friend’s recommendation. However, only a minority of people will browse the website before going to the activity fair. The mainly reason including they think it's too much and unnecessary, or some users don't know there is a website at all.
